James on a Mission in Space

Mission

Entering a whole new regime of astrophysics and discoveting a new frontier.

Vission

The observatory will study every phase of cosmic history from within our solar system to the most distant observable galaxies in the early universe.

Goals

The Telescope will explore the birth of the Universe using a technique called transmission spectroscopy, and search water and habitability on the surface of other planets. It will also observe exoplanets, solar system and so on.

Exploring Distant Worlds

and the Solar System

The James Telescope will be a powerful tool for studying the nearby universe. Scientists will use this observatory to study planets and other bodies in our solar system to determine their origin and evolution and compare them with exoplanets, planets that orbit other stars. This Telescope will also observe exoplanets located in their stars’ habitable zones, the regions where a planet could harbor liquid water on its surface, and can determine if and where signatures of habitability may be present.
